FAQ

Q: What information does a person need to get registered?

A:

  • Name
  • Address
  • Driver's license or last 4 digits of SSN

Q: Do you need to be 18 years old to register to vote?

A: No! So long as you will be 18 by election day (November 3, 2020), you can get registered.

Q: Do you need to be a citizen to get registered to vote?

A: Yes! If you are not a citizen of the United States, you are not eligible to get registered.

Q: Are there any other restrictions on getting registered?

A: Nope! As long as you are a citizen and will be 18 by November 3, 2020, you can get registered.

Q: Can you get registered to vote online?

A: Yes! Visit https://ova.elections.il.gov/Step0.aspx to register online.

Q: How can you determine if somebody is registered to vote or not?

A: Visit https://ova.elections.il.gov/RegistrationLookup.aspx and enter their name, birthday, the zipcode of where they would be registered and their street address (if you are checking somebody's registration for them, be sure to ask for this information in advance).

Q: Do you need to re-register between elections?

A: Typically, no. The only time you need to re-register is when your permanent home address changes (e.g. when your family moves to a new city).
